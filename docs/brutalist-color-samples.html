Light Mode

Tailwind Slate on off-white — primary text 9.9:1 contrast

Typography Hierarchy

Heading XL (48px)
Heading L (36px)
Heading M (24px)
Heading S (19px)

Primary body text at 19px — comfortable for extended reading. This is what most document content looks like. The contrast ratio of 9.9:1 is well above WCAG AAA (7:1) without the eye strain of near-black on white.

Secondary text (slate-600, 7.2:1) — used for metadata, timestamps, file paths, and supporting information that should be readable but visually subordinate to primary content.

Muted text (slate-500, 4.6:1) — placeholders, captions, disabled states. Clearly lower priority in the visual hierarchy.

Here is a link in GOV.UK blue (#1d70b8) and a visited link in purple (#54319f). Links are always underlined in body content.

Surfaces & Borders

Surface (slate-100 #f1f5f9)
Sidebar, navigation panels, card backgrounds. Subtle distinction from page background.
Elevated Surface (white #ffffff)
Chat bubbles, modal cards, elevated content areas.

Inset text with left border — used for callouts, quotes, and supplementary information. The blue left border draws attention without overwhelming. This replaces decorative boxes and shadows.

Dark Mode

Tailwind Slate on deep blue-black — primary text 12.0:1 contrast

Typography Hierarchy

Heading XL (48px)
Heading L (36px)
Heading M (24px)
Heading S (19px)

Primary body text at 19px — comfortable for extended reading. This is what most document content looks like. The contrast ratio of 12.0:1 is slightly higher than light mode because dark backgrounds need more contrast to feel equally readable.

Secondary text (slate-400, 7.0:1) — used for metadata, timestamps, file paths, and supporting information that should be readable but visually subordinate to primary content.

Muted text (slate-500, 3.7:1) — placeholders, captions, disabled states. Clearly lower priority in the visual hierarchy.

Here is a link in lightened GOV.UK blue (#5694ca) and a visited link in lightened purple (#a594d4). Links are always underlined in body content.

Inset text with left border — used for callouts, quotes, and supplementary information. The blue left border draws attention without overwhelming. This replaces decorative boxes and shadows.
